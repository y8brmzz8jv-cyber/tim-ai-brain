# Mr. Pave — 90-Day Onboarding & Training Plan

Real, ready-to-use draft found in Drive — directly fulfills the 1-year goal of building "a documented sales system for asphalt, and a full sales & onboarding training program." This is the onboarding half.

## Core Principle (Small Crew)

Everyone must be able to do at least 2 roles well, step up/down depending on the job, and understand both asphalt and concrete fundamentals even if they specialize later.

**Typical crew breakdown:** Crew Lead/Operator, Primary Operator (roller/loader/concrete finishing), Skilled Laborer, Laborer, optional floater/truck driver.

## Days 1-14: Safety, Standards, & Both Trades

- Traffic control plans, flagging certification, PPE standards, concrete-specific hazards (silica, saw cutting)
- Asphalt basics: mix types, temperature/timing, compaction stages, edge/joint awareness
- Concrete basics: subgrade prep, forms/elevations, rebar/wire mesh/dowels, pour timing/finishing windows

**Milestone:** Safe around traffic, understands asphalt vs. concrete flow, follows instructions without slowing the crew.

## Days 15-30: Productive Laborer (Both Trades)

- Asphalt: raking behind paver, shoveling, edge work, plate compactor
- Concrete: form setup/stripping, screeding, bull floating, clean edges
- Equipment (observed → supervised): roller basics, skid steer spotting, saw cutting

**Milestone:** Can switch between asphalt & concrete days, maintains quality standards, keeps pace with a small crew.

## Days 31-60: Cross-Training & Equipment

- Asphalt: roller operation (breakdown & finish), joint density, handwork troubleshooting
- Concrete: finish timing, control joints, curing methods, ADA slopes/transitions (DOT/commercial)
- Equipment care: daily inspections, cleaning/greasing, reporting issues early

**Milestone:** Trusted with equipment, understands quality checkpoints, can fill in when someone's absent.

## Days 61-90: Specialization + Leadership Awareness

- **Path A (Asphalt-focused):** paver operation basics, screed awareness, truck exchange coordination
- **Path B (Concrete-focused):** advanced finishing, layout & grade control, small pour leadership
- **Leadership basics (everyone):** morning job brief, reading plans, communicating issues early

**90-Day Review:** Skill checklist updated, pay adjusted based on skills, clear next role defined.

## Skill Checklist (Ties to Pay Tiers)

- **Level 1 — Entry Laborer:** safe in traffic, PPE compliant, basic asphalt handwork, basic concrete labor, jobsite cleanup
- **Level 2 — Skilled Laborer (Cross-Trained):** raking/edging, form setup/screeding, plate compactor, spots equipment, switches trades seamlessly
- **Level 3 — Operator (Asphalt + Concrete):** roller (all stages), skid steer, saw cutting, concrete finishing, equipment inspections
- **Level 4 — Lead Operator/Assistant Lead:** paver basics, grade/slope awareness, ADA transitions, directs crew during pours/paves
- **Level 5 — Crew Lead:** runs job start to finish, handles DOT inspectors, maintains pace/quality, trains new hires

## Daily Crew Lead Checklist

- **Morning:** review scope/plans, traffic control in place, assign roles clearly, equipment checks
- **During work:** quality checks, adjust for weather/material, communicate with drivers & inspectors
- **End of day:** cleanup, equipment cleaning, review tomorrow's needs

## Residential / Commercial / DOT Differences

- **Residential:** homeowner communication, clean edges/appearance, driveway transitions
- **Commercial:** ADA compliance, drainage awareness, schedule coordination
- **DOT:** documentation, flagging/traffic control, inspector communication, zero shortcuts

## Core Values Rationale (for onboarding material / wall posters)

- **Quality:** "We take pride in doing the job right the first time. Quality means durable work, clean details, and results that last — not shortcuts that lead to callbacks. Our reputation is built on work we're willing to stand behind."
- **Safety:** "Everyone goes home healthy at the end of the day. Safety protects our people, the public, and the job. Working safely is not optional — it's how we operate, especially around traffic, equipment, and live jobsites."
- **Integrity:** "We do what we say we'll do, even when no one is watching. Integrity guides how we treat customers, inspectors, and each other. Honest work, fair decisions, and accountability build trust that lasts."
