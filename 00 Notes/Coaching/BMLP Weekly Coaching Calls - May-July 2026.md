Personal leadership coaching program through Cardone Ventures (same organization behind the DISC/R3 assessment and the 10X360 platform reviews Mr. Pave uses). These are personal development sessions, not company-specific action items — logged here rather than in a project Meetings folder.

## Key themes across sessions

**July 1 — Time execution & calendar-driven leadership:**
- Survey flagged time management and implementation as the top challenges for the group.
- Recommended: daily 30-min "do not schedule" reflection block; one top daily priority scheduled first; explicit due date + single accountable owner on every deliverable; weekly calendar audit; "island hours" for focused availability.
- Every deliverable needs a target date and one accountable stakeholder — directly reinforces the "never bury the ask" / "always end with a next action" rules already in your CLAUDE.md.

**June 24 — Leading AI-enabled transformation (Lucie Robinson):**
- Change management framed as a people problem, not a tech problem. Sequence: protect the core → create capacity → test ideas → scale/embed.
- "Stabilizers" (a DISC/R3 style) are a large, loyal group that resist rapid change and need safety + clear communication — worth remembering for your own team given the DISC mix at Mr. Pave.
- Modernized Eisenhower matrix for AI adoption: 2-factor grid of AI-readiness × business-impact — automate low-impact/high-readiness tasks; keep high-impact/low-readiness tasks human-led.

**June 3 — Leadership, scaling, and risk assessment:**
- "Four C's" framework: Courage → Confidence → Commitment → Conversion.
- Perfectionism flagged as a liability that slows speed — prioritize progress over perfect.
- On expansion: "optimize before expanding" — honestly assess if you can run multiple businesses/locations without degrading existing operations before taking on more (directly relevant to the Mr. Pave roll-up + regional expansion plans).
- On spouses working together: keep clear lanes of responsibility, avoid public disputes (relevant given Talita's role in Dantas Properties).

**May 27 — The "Four E's" framework:**
- Effective (do the right things) → Efficient (do them resourcefully) → Exemplify → Educate.
- "You can't improve what you don't measure" — tie KPIs to every initiative before rolling it out.

**May 13 — Decision-making frameworks & family-business leadership:**
- Coaching cases on family-business dynamics.
